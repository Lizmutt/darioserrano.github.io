Androgynous Spacecraft Docking Mechanism

Kinematics & Dynamics of Machines • Sep–Dec 2025

In a senior-level mechanics course I led the design of a rough-alignment docking interface suitable for small satellite applications. The mechanism needed to be androgynous, compact, and capable of transmitting significant axial forces without jamming. Deliverables included a fully parametric CAD model, MATLAB analysis code, and a detailed final report.

What I built

  • Six‑arm variable‑angle mechanism parametrized in SolidWorks to allow slider travel of 0.40–0.45 m and adjust the docking cone half-angle.
  • MATLAB scripts calculating kinematics and static equilibrium; solved a 6×6 system for joint reactions under a 6 kN axial docking load (≈1000 N per arm).
  • Jacobian-based force mapping to determine Stewart-platform‑style actuator loads and inform actuator selection.
  • FEA validation and parameter sweeps to ensure robustness across the motion range.

Key results

  • Designed to withstand a 6 kN axial load while maintaining alignment tolerances.
  • Calculated required slider actuator force of ≈2.17 kN, guiding motor sizing.

EEG Seizure Detection with CNN–LSTM

Applied Fundamentals of Deep Learning • May–Sep 2025

This project tackled seizure classification on pediatric EEG recordings (CHB‑MIT dataset). The goal was to build a deep learning pipeline that generalizes across patients by emphasizing robust preprocessing and evaluation on data the model had never seen.

What I built

  • End-to-end PyTorch CNN–LSTM architecture trained on multi-channel EEG windows.
  • Signal preprocessing including a 4th-order Butterworth bandpass filter, wavelet denoising, and overlapping sliding-window segmentation to boost data volume.
  • Training/validation split arranged by patient to measure true generalization; evaluation metrics computed on held‑out subjects.

Results

  • AUROC: 0.96 demonstrating strong discrimination ability.
  • F1-score: 0.84 on unseen patients, indicating balanced precision/recall.

Current Sensing & Anti-Aliasing PCB

Digital and Analog Circuits • Jan–May 2026

Designed and fabricated a two-layer printed circuit board to interface current sensing with high-speed ADC sampling, incorporating analog filtering and anti‑aliasing to support 50–70 kHz measurements.

  • Analog front-end using LM324 to map 0–3.5 A current to 0.25–3.3 V ADC range under a 0–19 V single-supply constraint.
  • Third-order Chebyshev low-pass filter (1 kHz cutoff, ≥ 35 dB attenuation at 5 kHz) designed in LTspice; validated DC gain and frequency response.
  • Layout in Eagle with careful power/ground return routing; generated Gerber/drill files and hand‑soldered prototype for validation.

Mechanical Engineering Design • Sep–Dec 2024

Designed a desktop CNC mill targeting hobbyists, balancing cost, rigidity, and ease of manufacture. The project delivered full 3D CAD models, component selection rationale, and a detailed cost–performance analysis.
